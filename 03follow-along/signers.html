Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

John Hancock
The first signer of the Declaration, known for his bold signature. Served as President of the Second Continental Congress and helped fund revolutionary efforts.
Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration and a brilliant political thinker who later became the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Inventor, diplomat, and scientist who secured French support during the Revolution and became one of the most influential Founding Fathers.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence, later the 2nd U.S. President, and a key negotiator of the peace treaty ending the Revolutionary War.
Samuel Adams
Organizer of early revolutionary resistance, including the Boston Tea Party, and a major force in rallying support for independence.
Richard Henry Lee
The delegate who officially proposed the resolution calling for independence, which led to drafting the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
A respected statesman known for practical solutions and one of the few men to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest colonists, known for advocating religious liberty and independence.
George Wythe
A prominent legal scholar and early anti-slavery advocate who taught Thomas Jefferson and other future leaders.
